High-performing professionals often seek support when burnout, sustained stress, or constant pressure begin to affect both performance and relationships.

Joseph brings a practical understanding of high-responsibility environments shaped by more than twenty years in public accounting, banking, and finance before transitioning to counseling. He was a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) and holds a Master of Business Administration (MBA). He also holds a Master of Arts in Counseling from Northwestern University and is a Licensed Clinical Professional Counselor (LCPC) in Illinois.

This background informs a direct, structured style that aligns with how professionals think, operate, and make decisions. The goal is not simply to reduce stress, but to restore clarity, consistency, sustainable performance, and a stronger sense of control.

Treatment centers on identifying the patterns that drive burnout and implementing targeted changes that support lasting improvement. Evidence-based methodologies including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, and psychodynamic therapy are integrated in a way that remains practical, relevant, and tailored to the individual.

When burnout begins to affect both work and home life, early attention can prevent deeper disruption.
